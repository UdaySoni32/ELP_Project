What is Cultured Meat?

Cultured meat is genuine animal meat produced by cultivating animal cells directly. This process allows for the creation of meat in a clean, controlled environment, offering a sustainable and ethical alternative to traditional livestock farming. The first cultured burger was created in 2013, and the technology has advanced rapidly since.

How It's Made

  1. Harmless Cell Sampling: A small sample of cells is taken from a living animal, without causing harm.
  2. Nutrient-Rich Growth: The cells are placed in a bioreactor and fed a rich broth of nutrients to encourage them to multiply and grow.
  3. Tissue Formation: The cells differentiate into muscle, fat, and connective tissue, forming the basis of the meat.
  4. Final Assembly: The harvested tissues are assembled into the final product, whether it's a burger, nugget, or fillet.

Benefits & Comparison

Cultured meat offers significant advantages over both conventional and even some plant-based alternatives.

Industry Leaders & Innovators

The alternative protein space is vibrant and growing, with companies specializing in cultured meat, plant-based products, and other novel innovations.

LG

Lab-Grown (Cultured)

Companies like Upside Foods and Mosa Meat are pioneering the production of real meat from cells.

PB

Plant-Based

Leaders like Beyond Meat and Impossible Foods use plant-derived ingredients to replicate conventional meat.

OI

Other Innovations

Brands like Quorn (mycoprotein) and Meati Foods (mycelium) create unique, protein-rich foods from fungi.
